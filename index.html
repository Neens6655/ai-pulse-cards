Editor's Analysis · Policy10 / 15

Four capitals, four playbooks for AI

June's rulebooks weren't one race — they were four different bets. Each bloc governs AI through the one lever it already holds. The executive read:

USGovern by leverage. Chip-export deals and executive orders — no broad federal law. Market-led, control at the border.
CNOpenness as industrial policy. Give the models away as open weights to win global reach past the chip bans.
INCompute as a public utility. State-subsidised GPUs for cheap access — buy your way to independence.
EUOne hard law. The AI Act — rights-first, horizontal, compliance biting from Aug 2.
The synthesisThe real axis isn't fast-vs-slow — it's control vs. diffusion. The US and EU tighten (export control, regulation); China and India diffuse (open weights, cheap compute).
The Gulf readYou can buy compute like India and attract models like China — but the moment you sell abroad you inherit America's controls and Europe's compliance. The edge goes to whoever deploys fastest inside that box.
